Rajtantra

  • Good Works:
    • Unified Nepal under King Prithvi Narayan Shah, creating a single nation from fragmented states.
    • Preserved Nepal’s sovereignty against British colonial threats during the Shah dynasty.
    • Promoted cultural heritage, including festivals, temples, and the Nepali language.
    • Built infrastructure like the Tribhuvan Highway and early hydropower projects under King Mahendra.
    • Established Nepal as a global tourism hub, leveraging royal patronage of Everest and heritage sites.
    • Provided decades of stability, avoiding the chaos of frequent government changes.
    • Supported rural education and health initiatives through royal trusts.
    • Symbolized national unity and pride, fostering a strong Nepali identity worldwide.
    • Established the Royal Nepal Academy to promote arts and culture.
    • Initiated land reform programs to benefit farmers.
  • Bad Works:
    • Accused of authoritarian rule (2002-2006), though some argue it was to curb instability.
    • Royal family faced allegations of mismanaging palace wealth.
    • Failed to modernize the economy, leading to stagnation.
    • Concentrated power within the royal family, limiting democratic participation.

Ganatantra

  • Good Works:
    • Ended monarchy in 2008, transitioning to a republic.
    • Some progress in road and hydropower projects.
    • Implemented policies to increase literacy rates.
    • Expanded infrastructure in rural areas.
    • Promoted gender equality through legislation.
    • Social Welfare Programs: Provided pensions, maternity benefits, and free vaccinations.
    • Diplomatic Relations: Maintained balanced ties with India, China, and international partners.
    • Women’s Rights: Ensured 33% female representation in parliament and legal protections
  • Bad Works & Corruption Allegations:
    • Widebody Airbus scandal squandered millions in taxpayer money.
    • Maoist insurgency killed over 17,000 people and destroyed infrastructure.
    • Nepotism and cronyism led to unqualified leaders dominating politics.
    • Frequent government collapses (over 10 PMs since 2008) stalled development.
    • Corruption in earthquake relief funds left victims homeless years later.
    • Bhutanese refugee scandal
